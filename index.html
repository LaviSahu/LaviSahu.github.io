01 The Work

Fourteen years of supply chain consulting means I've seen most of what can go right and wrong in a large implementation. My work lives at the intersection of advanced planning platforms, S&OP design, inventory optimisation, and AI in planning — usually in pharma, FMCG, utilities, or energy. At EY I lead technology engagements focused on making platforms actually work in practice, long after the consultants have left.

The platform is rarely the problem. The question is always whether the organisation is ready to use it.

02 Selected Engagements

Client names stay confidential — the work speaks in outcomes. Across these, my seat changes: sometimes PMO lead, sometimes functional architect, sometimes the person accountable for value showing up after go-live.

Global FMCG major — Inventory

Found €30M+ of excess inventory hiding in plain sight.

A network-wide inventory assessment for a personal-care giant across Europe and the US: right-sizing safety stocks, separating cycle stock from comfort stock, and simulating what a single day of lead-time reduction was worth across the European network — a number large enough to change the conversation from "inventory project" to "board topic".

Industrial-equipment major — Planning platform

The playbooks behind a global supply-allocation engine.

Designed the planning logic a global manufacturer runs its allocation decisions on: supply allocation, aggregate planning, dependent-demand forecasting with forecast-accuracy and bias dashboards, and MSS/RCCP master scheduling — plus a safety-stock methodology that corrects for forecast error, bias and supply reliability instead of pretending demand is well-behaved.

Energy major — S&OP / S&OE on Kinaxis

An operating blueprint, not a slide deck.

End-to-end S&OP and S&OE design on RapidResponse — planning calendar, decision rights, exception workflows — built with the explicit test that it should survive contact with the organisation long after the engagement team rolls off. It did.
